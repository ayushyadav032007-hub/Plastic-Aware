RECYCLING

Plastic Recycling Guide

Learn how plastic recycling works and how you can make a difference by recycling correctly.

Why Recycling Matters

Plastic recycling helps reduce pollution, conserve resources, and decrease the amount of plastic waste ending up in landfills and oceans. However, only a small percentage of plastic is actually recycled worldwide.

Understanding how recycling works and sorting plastic correctly can dramatically increase recycling efficiency and protect our planet.

Plastic Recycling Codes

Most plastic products have a number inside a triangle symbol. These numbers identify the type of plastic and determine whether it can be recycled.

Code Plastic Type Common Uses Recyclable?
1 PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) Water bottles, soda bottles Yes
2 HDPE (High Density Polyethylene) Milk jugs, detergent bottles Yes
3 PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) Pipes, medical tubing Rarely
4 LDPE (Low Density Polyethylene) Plastic bags, food wraps Sometimes
5 PP (Polypropylene) Food containers, bottle caps Yes (in many areas)
6 PS (Polystyrene) Foam cups, takeaway boxes Rarely
7 Other Mixed plastics, bioplastics Difficult

How Plastic Recycling Works

1. Collection

Plastic waste is collected from homes, recycling bins, and waste centers.

2. Sorting

Machines and workers separate plastics by type and color.

3. Cleaning

Plastics are washed to remove labels, food residue, and contaminants.

4. Shredding

The plastic is shredded into small flakes or pellets.

5. Melting

The flakes are melted and molded into raw material for new products.

6. Manufacturing

Recycled plastic is used to produce new items like bottles, containers, and fabrics.

Tips for Recycling at Home

✔ Rinse containers before putting them in recycling bins.

✔ Remove food waste and liquids from plastic packaging.

✔ Do not place plastic bags in regular recycling bins.

✔ Check recycling symbols before disposal.

✔ Avoid mixing recyclables with general trash.

Be Part of the Solution

Recycling is one of the easiest ways individuals can reduce plastic pollution. By making small changes in daily habits, we can collectively reduce the environmental impact of plastic waste.
